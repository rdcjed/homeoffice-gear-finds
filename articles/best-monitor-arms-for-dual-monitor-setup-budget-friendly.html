If you're using two monitors without arms, you're wasting desk space and putting your neck at risk. Monitor arms free up valuable desk real estate, improve ergonomics by letting you position screens at eye level, and make your setup look clean with built-in cable management.

We tested 10 dual monitor arms for stability, range of motion, build quality, and ease of installation. Here are the best budget-friendly options.

Gas Spring vs Fixed Arm: Which One?

Gas spring arms offer smooth height adjustment with a simple touch — great if you frequently switch between sitting and standing desks. Fixed arms are cheaper and more stable but require manual adjustment with a hex key.

Installation Tips

Most monitor arms use a C-clamp (edges of desk) or grommet mount (hole in desk). C-clamp is easier and works for most desks. Make sure your desk can support the clamp and that the monitor arm's weight rating covers both of your monitors combined.
