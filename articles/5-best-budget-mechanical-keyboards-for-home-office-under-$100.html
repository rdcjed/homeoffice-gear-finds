What to Look for in a Budget Mechanical Keyboard

Switch Type Matters

For office work, we recommend linear switches (like Cherry MX Red or Gateron Red) for quiet operation, or tactile switches (Brown) if you want feedback without the noise. Avoid clicky switches (Blue) if you share an office space or take frequent calls.

Layout Considerations

Full-size (100%) keyboards have all keys including numpad. TKL (Tenkeyless, 80%) drops the numpad for more mouse space. 60% keyboards remove the function row and arrow keys for maximum desk space. For most home office workers, a TKL or 75% layout hits the sweet spot.

Connectivity

Wireless keyboards with Bluetooth 5.0+ offer the most flexibility, allowing you to switch between your laptop, tablet, and phone. However, wired keyboards are typically cheaper and have zero input lag.
