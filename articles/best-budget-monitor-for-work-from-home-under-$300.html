A good monitor is the centerpiece of any home office. After spending 8+ hours staring at a screen, the difference between a decent monitor and a great one is night and day — both for your productivity and your eye health.

We evaluated monitors on resolution, color accuracy, connectivity (especially USB-C), ergonomics (height adjustable stands), and eye-care features. Here are the best monitors under $300 for remote workers.

Why USB-C Connectivity Matters

A monitor with USB-C power delivery is a game-changer. With a single cable, you can connect your laptop to the monitor, charge it, and connect peripherals through the monitor's USB hub. Look for monitors with at least 60W USB-C PD.

4K vs QHD vs 1080p: Which Resolution?

For most home office workers, a 27-inch 4K monitor offers the best balance of screen real estate and sharpness. QHD (2560x1440) is also excellent and generally cheaper. 1080p is fine for 24-inch screens but starts to look pixelated at 27 inches.
